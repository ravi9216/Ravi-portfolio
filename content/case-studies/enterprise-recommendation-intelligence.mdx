## Architecting Quote-to-Order into a Real-Time Recommendation System

Transformed a legacy, low-trust quote-to-order system into a real-time recommendation platform, reducing decision time by 50%, increasing conversion by 21%, and cutting onboarding costs by 80% through API-first architecture, hybrid AI models, and a reusable experimentation framework.

Problem

The quote-to-order system represented a critical revenue bottleneck:

Batch Pricing Logic → Delayed decision-making in high-velocity sales cycles

Manual XML Integrations → 6-month onboarding cycles limiting scalability

Low Trust in Recommendations → Sales teams overrode outputs >60% of the time

⚠️ Core Platform Gap:
The system lacked a real-time, trustworthy decisioning layer, preventing scale across pricing, quoting, and sales workflows.

This wasn’t a pricing problem—it was a decisioning architecture + trust problem

Customer Insights
1. Override Behavior = Broken Trust Signal

Behavioral data showed:

Sales reps consistently ignored recommendations

Manual adjustments were the norm

Insight: Adoption failure was not due to model accuracy—but lack of explainability and control

2. Latency Killed Conversion

Friction logs revealed:

Delayed pricing → lost deals

Static quotes → poor responsiveness to customer context

Insight: In sales workflows, speed is a competitive advantage

3. Integration Friction Blocked Growth

XML-based onboarding required heavy engineering effort

Each new client → months of setup

Insight: Growth was constrained by integration complexity, not demand
